How to Simplify Expressions

Explanation

To simplify algebraic expressions:

  1. Identify like terms - terms with the same variable and exponent
  2. Combine coefficients of like terms by adding or subtracting
  3. Keep the variable part unchanged
  4. Write terms in alphabetical order as a convention

Example: 2x + 7x = (2+7)x = 9x

Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Don't combine terms with different variables (e.g., 3x + 2y cannot be combined)
  • Watch the signs when subtracting (e.g., 5x - 8x = -3x, not 3x)
  • Don't forget to include the variable in your answer
  • Remember that terms without variables (constants) can only combine with other constants
