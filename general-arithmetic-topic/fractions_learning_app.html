Understanding the Building Blocks of Math

1. The Concept of a "Whole"

What is a Whole?

A fraction is always part of a whole. The "whole" is the complete thing we start with before dividing it into parts. The whole can be:

  • A pizza üçï
  • A chocolate bar üç´
  • A shape (circle, rectangle)
  • A distance on a number line (from 0 to 1)

2. Language & Notation

Understanding the Numbers

Every fraction has two numbers:

3
4

Numerator (top): How many parts we have or are counting (3 parts)

Denominator (bottom): How many equal parts the whole is divided into (4 equal parts)

Reading and Writing Fractions

Examples of how to say fractions:

12 = "one half" or "one over two"

14 = "one quarter" or "one fourth"

13 = "one third"

23 = "two thirds"

38 = "three eighths"
