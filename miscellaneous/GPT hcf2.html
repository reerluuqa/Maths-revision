What you’re practising

HCF (GCD) of several numbers is the largest integer that divides each number with no remainder.

To find HCF of 3+ numbers, compute it pairwise: HCF(a,b,c) = HCF(HCF(a,b), c).

Fast methods you’ll see

  • Prime factorisation: keep only primes common to all, using the smallest powers.
  • Euclidean algorithm: repeated remainders; very fast for large values.

Common pitfalls (avoid these)

  • Stopping at “a common factor” (like 2) instead of the greatest.
  • For prime factors, using the largest powers (that’s for LCM), not the smallest.
  • For 3–4 numbers, finding HCF of only two and forgetting to check the rest.
  • When the HCF is 1, it means the numbers are coprime as a set — that’s valid.

Mini-check

If your answer is the HCF, it must divide every number exactly. Quick test: divide each number mentally and ensure remainder = 0.
